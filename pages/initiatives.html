Youth Sports & Talent Development

Engaging youth in constructive activities, nurturing talent, and combating substance abuse

Our youth sports programs are designed to keep young people engaged in positive activities while developing their athletic abilities and building character. Through regular tournaments and training sessions, we provide alternatives to negative influences and create pathways for talent development.

Manda-Shivanga Super Cup

Annual football tournament bringing together youth from across the ward for healthy competition and talent scouting.

  • 16+ teams participating annually
  • Professional coaching and mentorship
  • Talent identification for higher levels

Talent Scouting Programs

Systematic identification and nurturing of athletic abilities in various sports including football, athletics, and volleyball.

  • Regular talent identification camps
  • Partnerships with sports academies
  • Scholarship opportunities for gifted athletes

Sports Equipment Support

Providing essential sports kits and equipment to community teams to ensure they can participate fully in competitions.

  • Uniforms and training gear
  • Sports equipment for schools
  • Facility maintenance support

Impact

500+ Youth Engaged
20+ Teams Supported
15 Tournaments Held

Housing & Welfare Support

Providing decent housing for the most vulnerable community members

Everyone deserves a safe and dignified place to call home. Our housing initiatives focus on providing shelter to the most vulnerable members of our community, including widows, elderly persons, and people with disabilities.

House Construction for Needy Families

Building complete homes for vulnerable families who cannot afford decent housing, providing them with security and dignity.

  • 2-bedroom houses with basic amenities
  • Solar lighting installation
  • Rainwater harvesting systems

Mama Grace Mwanje Housing Project

Complete house construction and furnishing for widow support, serving as a model for vulnerable family assistance.

  • Fully furnished house
  • Kitchen and bathroom facilities
  • Perimeter fencing for security

Home Improvement Initiative

Renovating existing structures for vulnerable families to improve living conditions and safety.

  • Roof repairs and replacements
  • Flooring and wall improvements
  • Window and door installations

Impact

50+ Houses Built
200+ People Sheltered
15 Home Improvements

Social Welfare Activities

Enhancing community health and social protection

Health is wealth, and we're committed to ensuring every resident of Manda-Shivanga Ward has access to quality healthcare services. Our social welfare programs focus on preventive care, health education, and access to medical services.

Free Medical Camps

Regular health check-ups and treatment services provided in partnership with local healthcare professionals.

  • General health screenings
  • Basic medical treatments
  • Health education sessions

SHA Registration Drives

Facilitating Social Health Authority enrollment to ensure community members have health insurance coverage.

  • Registration assistance
  • Premium payment support
  • Education on benefits

Community Health Outreach

Mobile health services reaching remote areas and underserved populations within the ward.

  • Mobile clinic services
  • Vaccination campaigns
  • Maternal health programs

Impact

1000+ Patients Treated
500+ SHA Registrations
25 Medical Camps

Education Support & Bursaries

Ensuring educational access for children from vulnerable families

Education is the key to breaking the cycle of poverty. We provide comprehensive support to ensure that financial constraints never prevent a child from accessing quality education and reaching their full potential.

Kivishi Foundation Bursaries

Financial support for secondary and tertiary education, covering school fees, examination fees, and essential learning materials.

  • Full and partial bursaries
  • Merit-based and need-based awards
  • Continuous academic support

School Supplies Distribution

Providing essential learning materials including books, uniforms, and stationery to students from vulnerable backgrounds.

  • Textbooks and learning materials
  • School uniforms and shoes
  • Mathematical sets and stationery

Mentorship and Career Guidance

Supporting students in making informed academic and career choices through regular mentorship sessions.

  • Career counseling sessions
  • Academic performance monitoring
  • Role model programs

Impact

200+ Bursaries Awarded
1000+ Students Supported
95% Completion Rate

Infrastructure Support

Improving physical infrastructure for community development

Good infrastructure is the backbone of community development. We work to improve roads, water systems, and public facilities to enhance connectivity, access to services, and overall quality of life for residents.

Road Maintenance Partnerships

Collaborating with local organizations and sugar factories to maintain and improve road networks across the ward.

  • Gravel road maintenance
  • Drainage construction
  • Bridge repairs and construction

Community Facility Development

Markets, health centers, and public spaces to enhance community interaction and service delivery.

  • Market stalls construction
  • Health center improvements
  • Public seating areas

Water and Sanitation Projects

Clean water access and waste management systems to improve public health and environmental conditions.

  • Borehole drilling
  • Water kiosk construction
  • Toilet facilities in public areas

Impact

30km Roads Improved
10 Water Points
15 Public Facilities

Empowerment of Women & Youth

Economic empowerment through entrepreneurship and skills development

Economic empowerment is essential for sustainable community development. We focus on building capacity among women and youth to create income-generating opportunities and reduce dependency.

Community Fundraisers

Supporting women's groups and youth enterprises through organized fundraising events and seed capital provision.

  • Business startup capital
  • Equipment purchases
  • Market linkage support

Self-Help Group Support

Capacity building and financial literacy training for community-based organizations and savings groups.

  • Financial management training
  • Group dynamics workshops
  • Monitoring and evaluation support

Skills Development Workshops

Vocational training in various trades including tailoring, carpentry, hairdressing, and modern agriculture.

  • Technical skills training
  • Business management courses
  • Apprenticeship programs

Impact

100+ Businesses Started
500+ People Trained
25 Self-Help Groups
